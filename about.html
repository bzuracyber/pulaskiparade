About the Parade & Our Hero

The history of General Casimir Pulaski and the General Casimir Pulaski Memorial Parade Committee.

The Man

General Casimir Pulaski

Father of the American Cavalry & Honorary American Citizen

Casimir Pulaski was born in March 1748, the oldest son of Count Joseph Pulaski. At just 15 years old, he joined his father in opposing Russian and Prussian interference in Polish affairs. When Poland was partitioned, he was outlawed by Russia and forced to flee.

Pulaski traveled to Paris, where he met Benjamin Franklin, who convinced him to support the American colonies in their fight for independence. Franklin wrote to General George Washington, describing Pulaski as "an officer, renowned throughout Europe for the courage and bravery he displayed in defense of his country's freedom."

In 1777, Pulaski arrived in Philadelphia and met General Washington. He quickly distinguished himself at the Battle of Brandywine, and Congress appointed him Brigadier-General in charge of Four Horse Brigades. He later commanded the cavalry at Germantown and Valley Forge.

In 1778, Congress approved the establishment of the American Cavalry with Pulaski at its head, earning him the title "Father of the American Cavalry." Pulaski went so far as to use his own personal funds to equip his soldiers.

On October 9, 1779, Pulaski was mortally wounded during the Siege of Savannah, Georgia. He died two days later on October 11, 1779.

On November 7, 2009, President Barack Obama signed a joint resolution making Pulaski an honorary American citizen — only the eighth person in American history to receive this honor — 230 years after his death.

Today, parks, streets, statues, and schools across the United States bear his name as a lasting tribute to his courage and sacrifice.

About the GPMPC

General Casimir Pulaski Memorial Parade Committee, Inc.

Our Mission

The General Casimir Pulaski Memorial Parade Committee, Inc. (GPMPC) organizes the annual Pulaski Day Parade on 5th Avenue in New York City — one of the most celebrated ethnic parades in the world's greatest city.

We celebrate the memory of General Casimir Pulaski, the Polish-American community, and the enduring friendship between Poland and the United States of America.
