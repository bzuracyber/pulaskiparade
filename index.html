Parade Leadership

Meet Our Committee

The General Casimir Pulaski Memorial Parade Committee is led by dedicated individuals committed to celebrating Polish-American heritage and culture. Our leadership ensures the success of the 89th Annual Pulaski Day Parade every October.

From organizing march participants to coordinating Grand Marshal ceremonies and managing the annual journal, our committee members work tirelessly year-round to make this celebration memorable.

Our Hero

General Casimir Pulaski

Father of the American Cavalry & Honorary American Citizen

Casimir Pulaski was born in March 1748, the oldest son of Count Joseph Pulaski. At just 15, he joined his father opposing Russian and Prussian interference in Polish affairs.

In Paris, Benjamin Franklin convinced him to support the American colonies — writing to George Washington that Pulaski was "an officer, renowned throughout Europe for the courage and bravery he displayed in defense of his country's freedom."

In 1778, Congress established the American Cavalry with Pulaski at its head, earning him the title "Father of the American Cavalry." He used his own personal funds to equip his soldiers.

He died October 11, 1779, from wounds suffered at the Battle of Savannah. On November 7, 2009, President Barack Obama signed a resolution making him an honorary American citizen — 230 years after his death.

Key Dates

1748 Born in Poland, son of Count Joseph Pulaski
1777 Arrived in Philadelphia, met General Washington; distinguished at Battle of Brandywine
