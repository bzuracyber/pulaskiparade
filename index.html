The Story

General Casimir Pulaski

Born in March 1748 in the Mazovia region of Poland, Casimir Pulaski grew up in a family deeply committed to Polish liberty. By the age of 15 he was already fighting against Russian interference in his homeland, joining the Bar Confederation — a noble uprising dedicated to preserving Polish sovereignty and independence.

Forced into exile, Pulaski eventually made his way to Paris, where he met Benjamin Franklin. Franklin was so impressed that he wrote personally to General Washington: "an officer renowned throughout Europe for his courage and bravery." That letter opened the door to America's revolutionary cause.

Arriving in Philadelphia in 1777, Pulaski was appointed Brigadier-General of Cavalry by the Continental Congress, earning him the enduring title Father of the American Cavalry. He gave his life for American freedom at the Battle of Savannah on October 11, 1779. In 2009, President Obama posthumously granted him honorary American citizenship — one of only eight people in history to receive that distinction.

2026 Grand Marshal · 89th Annual Parade

Dr. Iwona Korga

President & Executive Director, Joseph Piłsudski Institute of America
Treasurer, PSFCU Board of Directors

Dr. Korga is a distinguished leader in the Polish-American community, serving in prominent roles at two of Polonia's most important institutions. She was honored at Polish Heritage Night at Gracie Mansion alongside Zygmunt Bielski, Parade Director, by the NYC Mayor.

Her decades of service to the Piłsudski Institute have preserved irreplaceable archives and historical materials for future generations of Polish-Americans, and her financial leadership at the Polish and Slavic Federal Credit Union continues to strengthen our community's economic foundation.
